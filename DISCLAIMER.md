## Data Authority and Limitations

### Source Authority

This Tool aggregates data from multiple international sources with varying levels of authority:

| Source | Authority Level | Use Case |
|--------|----------------|----------|
| **WHO Constitution** | Treaty (binding on member states) | Foundational health governance |
| **IHR (2005)** | International treaty (binding) | Health emergencies, notifications |
| **WHO FCTC** | International treaty (binding) | Tobacco control obligations |
| **ICH Guidelines** | Harmonized guidance (non-binding, widely adopted) | Pharmaceutical development |
| **IMDRF N-series** | Harmonized guidance (non-binding, widely adopted) | Medical device regulation |
| **Declaration of Helsinki** | Ethical code (non-binding, referenced by regulators) | Medical research ethics |
| **Codex Alimentarius** | International standards (reference standards for WTO) | Food safety |

**CRITICAL**: ICH and IMDRF guidance documents are **not legally binding** in themselves. National regulators adopt, adapt, or reference them — always check the applicable national law.

### Coverage Gaps

This Tool does **NOT** include:

1. **National implementing legislation** — Only international instruments, not how individual countries implement them
2. **TRIPS Agreement** — Pharmaceutical IP aspects not covered
3. **Regional frameworks** — EU MDR/IVDR, Pan American Health Organization instruments, Africa CDC frameworks
4. **Court decisions** — No case law from international courts or tribunals
5. **IHR 2024 amendments** — Recent revisions may not be reflected
6. **Full standard text for Codex** — Registry metadata and links only; full standard text not extracted
